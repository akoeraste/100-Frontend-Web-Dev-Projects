The Legend

From Abidjan to the World

Born on January 26, 1986 in Yopougon, Abidjan, Ange Didier Houon — known to the world as DJ Arafat — would become the most influential figure in Coupé-Décalé music and one of Africa's brightest stars.

The son of a singer mother, music was woven into his DNA. He began DJing as a teenager in the nightclubs of Abidjan, quickly mastering the turntables before transitioning into production and performance. His raw energy, technical skill, and fearless showmanship set him apart from the start.

By his early twenties, DJ Arafat had transformed Coupé-Décalé from a local dance movement into a continental phenomenon — earning him the title "Commandant Zabra" and later, the crown that would define his legacy: "Le Roi du Coupé-Décalé" — the undisputed King.

The Journey

A Life in Rhythm

1986

Born in Yopougon

Ange Didier Houon is born in Yopougon, a vibrant commune of Abidjan, Côte d'Ivoire. Raised by his mother, singer Tina Glamour, music surrounds him from birth.

2003

Rise of the DJ

Begins performing in Abidjan's hottest nightclubs, earning a reputation as a fearless DJ with unmatched energy behind the decks. The name "DJ Arafat" starts circulating through the city.

2005

Jonathan

Releases "Jonathan," his breakout track that becomes an anthem across West Africa. The song marks his transition from DJ to full-blown artist and cultural force.

2013

Kpangor

Drops "Kpangor" — a track that ignites a dance craze across the continent and beyond. The song solidifies his status as the King of Coupé-Décalé and wins multiple awards.

2016

Commandant Zabra

Releases the hit "Commandant Zabra," further cementing his dominance. Performs sold-out concerts across Africa and Europe, filling arenas with tens of thousands of fans.

2019

A Star Goes Home

On August 12, 2019, DJ Arafat passes away following a motorcycle accident in Abidjan. He is 33 years old. Tens of thousands gather for his funeral — a testament to the love he inspired.

The Legacy

More Than Music

He didn't just make music — he created a movement, a culture, a way of life.

The Sound

DJ Arafat redefined Coupé-Décalé with aggressive beats, raw vocals, and a fusion of electronic and traditional Ivorian rhythms that made dance floors tremble.

The Energy

His live shows were legendary — electric performances where he commanded stages like a general commands an army. Every concert was an unforgettable experience.

The Movement

He built "La Chine" — a devoted fanbase named after a superpower. His followers weren't just fans; they were a family, a nation united by rhythm and loyalty.

The Influence

From Abidjan to Paris, Kinshasa to London — DJ Arafat carried Ivorian music to the world stage and inspired a generation of African artists who followed.
